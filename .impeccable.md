# AYBKK Mission Control — Design Context

## Users
Yoga practitioners across four communities: Russia (Saint Petersburg/Moscow workshop), China (Huizhou + Guangzhou + in-depth Mysore), Bangkok shala regulars. They use the journal app on phones immediately after class, and again later to view their history or share a card to WeChat / Telegram. Audience skews anti-tech, lineage-respecting, intermediate-to-advanced practice level.

## Brand Personality
Three words: warm, traditional, lineage-respecting. The product is the work of Boonchu Tanti (老师, KPJAYI lineage). It must never feel SaaS, AI-generated, or like a wellness startup. Closer to: a hand-typeset mantra card, a museum exhibit caption, a yoga teacher's printed manual from the Mysore tradition.

## Aesthetic Direction
- **Editorial / lineage / refined**: think Production Type magazines, KPJAYI archive prints, classical Indian text editions — not gradient blue dashboards.
- **Photo-first**: students upload one face photo. The photo is the soul of the share card — it must show through clearly, not be drowned in tint.
- **Day-of-week pastel theme**: existing color system uses 7 calibrated pastels (sage / terracotta / gold / orange / dusty blue / lavender / mist) keyed to entry's day. Keep this — it's distinctive.
- **Multilingual native**: zh / en / th / ru everywhere. Typography must work for Latin AND CJK AND Cyrillic.

## Anti-references
- AI-slop tells: gradient text, glassmorphism, neon-on-dark, generic rounded-card stacks, Inter/Roboto-as-everything.
- "Cool tech" tropes: dark mode glow, 3-color gradients, big icon above heading.
- Wellness-app pastels with sans-serif everywhere → reads like Calm or Headspace, which Boonchu's audience explicitly is not.

## Design Principles
1. **Photo is the soul.** Tint exists for legibility, not aesthetic. If face isn't visible, fix it.
2. **Hierarchy through typography, not just size.** Vary family / weight / style / case to create real visual rhythm.
3. **Quiet > loud.** Restraint reads as authority. Boonchu's voice is direct, never salesy.
4. **Lineage signals.** Sutra and Bhagavad Gita quotes belong. So does Sanskrit rendering. So does serif typography that feels printed, not pixel-perfect.
5. **Mobile-first, always.** 480px max-width is the design canvas for screens; 640×720 PNG is the canvas for share cards.

## Typography stack (current)
- Body / UI: Inter (banned by impeccable but currently load-bearing across many pages — replace gradually, not in a single sweep).
- Share card: Spectral (Google Fonts, Production Type) — refined editorial serif with italics. Used in 700 (name), 600 (uplift), 500 (eyebrow caps), 400 italic (date/quote), 300 italic (byline). Established 2026-04-27.
